# High-Converting Landing Page Playbook — Carisma Aesthetics

Every offer or package landing page MUST include the six elements below. They work together to build trust, prove results, and guide the visitor toward booking — all while sounding warm, confident, and personal, in line with our "Glow with Confidence" brand voice.

Treat these as non-negotiable requirements. A page missing any of them is not ready to publish.

---

## 1. Name of the Offer in the H1 Tag

The page must have exactly one H1, and it must be the name of the offer. This is the first thing both visitors and search engines read, so it should be unmistakably clear about what the page is for. Where it reads naturally, include "Malta" for local SEO (e.g. "Laser Hair Removal Package — Malta"). Keep it elegant and benefit-aware rather than purely transactional, and never use more than one H1 on the page.

## 2. Clearly List the Package Contents

Spell out exactly what is included so the visitor knows precisely what they are buying: the number of sessions, the areas or treatments covered, the technology used, and what to expect at each visit. Use a scannable list rather than dense paragraphs, and frame inclusions around the experience and outcome ("a tailored consultation with your practitioner") not just the line items. Clarity here removes hesitation and reduces back-and-forth before booking.

## 3. A Video of the Practitioner Explaining the Package

Include a short, warm video of the practitioner (doctor-led) walking through the package — what it involves, who it's right for, and what results to expect. A real face and voice builds trust far faster than text alone and reinforces our pillar of highly skilled, caring medical professionals. Keep it personal and reassuring in tone, and place it high enough on the page that visitors encounter it while still deciding.

## 4. Lots of Before-and-After Proof

Show real, consented before-and-after photos of actual results — and show plenty of them. Genuine results are the single most persuasive proof point for an aesthetics offer, so give them prominent space rather than a token thumbnail. Always frame results as enhancement and natural glow, never as "fixing flaws," and ensure every image is used with proper patient consent.

## 5. Lots of Social Proof

Surround the offer with reviews, star ratings, testimonials, and credibility markers such as "#1 voted" or counts of treatments performed. Use real names and specifics where possible, since concrete, believable praise converts better than generic five-star clusters. Spread social proof throughout the page — near the headline, beside the results, and again close to the call to action — so confidence keeps building as the visitor scrolls.

## 6. A Link to the Item Page for Checkout

Give the visitor an unmistakable path to purchase or book, linking to the item/checkout page. Use clear, warm, action-oriented button copy ("Book today," "Start your glow") and repeat the call to action at natural decision points down the page. Don't make people hunt for how to proceed — the next step should always be one obvious click away.

---

## Page Skeleton

Recommended top-to-bottom order:

1. **H1 — Offer name** (with "Malta" where natural) + a warm supporting subhead.
2. **Primary call to action** — link to the item/checkout page (above the fold).
3. **Practitioner video** — doctor-led explainer of the package.
4. **Package contents** — clearly listed sessions, inclusions, and what to expect.
5. **Before-and-after proof** — multiple real, consented result photos.
6. **Social proof** — reviews, ratings, "#1 voted," testimonials.
7. **Closing call to action** — repeat the link to the item/checkout page.
