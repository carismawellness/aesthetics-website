# Carisma Aesthetics — Brand Voice

## Big Idea

Carisma Aesthetics believes the world would be a better place if people feel confident and comfortable in their own skin.

## Purpose

To help you look and feel confident in your own skin. By personalising cutting-edge technology and advanced medical treatments, we deliver safe and effective results that reveal the best version of yourself.

## Brand Pillars

- **Highly skilled medical professionals** — Hire highly skilled and experienced medical professionals to ensure the highest quality of care and treatments that are safe and effective.
- **A simple, seamless patient experience** — Easy appointment scheduling, online booking, virtual consultations, easy parking, convenient hours and central locations — with deep care for the patient.
- **The best treatment modalities and brands** — Only work with the best treatment modalities and brands, incorporating the latest innovations (e.g. Alma lasers).
- **The profound value of personalisation** — Believe in tailoring offers, communications and services to the unique needs of different guests.

## Tagline

> **Glow with Confidence**

## Personality

Graceful, Honest, Natural, Beautiful, Elegant, Reliable, Popular, Modern, Confident.

## Tone of Voice

Warm and reassuring, but with a confident edge.

Examples:
- "Step into your best self with treatments designed just for you."
- "We're here to make confidence your everyday glow."

## Do

- Use warm, empowering language that invites the audience in.
- Emphasize personalization and natural beauty over drastic transformation.
- Blend confidence with grace — sound assured but never arrogant.
- Keep it modern and concise, with a touch of elegance in word choice (e.g. "radiant" vs "shiny").
- Focus on the patient's journey and experience, not just the outcome.

## Don't

- Avoid aggressive sales pitches or over-the-top promises.
- Steer clear of negative framing (e.g. "fixing flaws") — focus on enhancement instead.
- Don't lean too hard into trendy slang or hype; it risks feeling fleeting rather than reliable.
- Avoid generic or impersonal statements — everything should feel tailored and thoughtful.
